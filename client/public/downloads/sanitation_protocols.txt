SMART HEALTH NORTHEAST - STANDARD SANITATION PROTOCOLS

CAUTIONS:
1. Open defecation is a primary cause of water-borne disease outbreaks.
2. Avoid handling food without washing hands with soap.
3. Keep latrines at least 30 meters away from water sources.

PREVENTION STEPS:
1. HAND WASHING: Wash hands with soap and water for 20 seconds before eating and after using the toilet.
2. OFFSITE DISPOSAL: Dispose of child feces immediately in a latrine.
3. WASTE MANAGEMENT: Burn or bury solid waste; do not dump it in rivers or drains.
4. FOOD HYGIENE: Wash raw vegetables and fruits with treated water before consumption.

COMMUNITY ACTION:
Report blocked drains or overflow issues via the 'Report' feature on the Smart Health portal.
